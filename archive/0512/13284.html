I would define freedom as a complete degree of separateness from any other objective structure or will. In that sense, there is no such thing as "free will" for humans or any other sentient beings save G-d. For humans to have free will, we would have complete autonomy from everything including the atoms in our bodies. Ultimately, we can not separate our will from the -illions of processes which occur to sustain our existence. On a much grander scale, individual humans do not possess a true free will completely separate from the other humans in society. Our choices and decisions are limited by the availability of resources and the choices of humans who existed before us. We are "limited will" beings. The only being capable of free will would exist existentially from our reality, say the programmer of the universe we live in for example. :)
