> I seek for omniscience, so that I would know everything, and that there would >be no things new to me, and that the time would stop existing to me (because of >absence of new things), and that is my preferred way to stop existing. You could accomplish that goal by reprogramming your goals so that you no longer had a desire to learn. Then you would know everything you wanted to know. > We are not sure weather our universe size is really finite or infinite. It is finite according to our current understanding of physics. We used to believe it was infinite because we couldn't see the boundaries. Now we know that the observable universe has a finite age, size, and mass according to the Big Bang theory, and finite resolution due to quantum mechanics. We now know there is an upper bound on the information content of the observable universe, namely the Bekenstein bound of the Hubble radius, 2.91 x 10^122 bits. No agent can know the exact state of any universe of which it is a part because that universe including the agent must have more possible states than the agent could represent. So the agent must always have some uncertainty about its environment. It can never know everything.
