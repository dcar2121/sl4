I think Tennessee's comments are right on the money. The whole determinism
vs. indeterminism is a red herring. Freedom is most definitely not a
correlative of indeterminacy. Which is the most free: a rational man who's
actions are mostly predictable or a crazy who acts randomly? The answer is
clear in my mind. Determinism is a fact - otherwise science (which seeks to
explicate the rules governing the cosmos) would not be possible. Quantum
indeterminacy is merely epistemological. The real debate is between
compatibalism and incompatiblism of "freedom" and determinism.
If the author of that times article actually read some Thomas Aquinas or
Occam instead of ridiculing medieval philosophy he would understand that
this question boils down to metaphysics and logic, i.e. the De Dicto/
De Redistinction.
