--- Jeff Herrlich <jeff_herrlich@yahoo.com> wrote:

> The peer-AGI idea is interesting and not impossible, in light of your view,
> do you believe that we should move forward with OpenCog, or not? (I presume
> that you believe we should move forward).

Yes. I don't believe it can get big enough to be dangerous. First, it lacks
a mechanism to defend against rogue agents. Second, from my limited
understanding of its architecture, modules communicate by writing to shared
atom tables (rather than natural language), which precludes a hybrid
carbon-silicon architecture.
