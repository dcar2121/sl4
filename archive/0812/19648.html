I think you need a more detailed model. Even were there a pocket of
Jupiter in which Deuterium were sufficiently concentrated, that wouldn't
apply to *most* of the planet. So even were that pocket to be ignited,
it wouldn't cause the planet to explode, but only that one small
pocket. (Even so, I doubt that the pocket could be exploded. Fusion
reactions generally require pressure and heat as well as the proper
ingredients.)

You should probably pick something else to worry about...or as a hook on
which to hang your story. MacroLife (George Zebrowski) did something
analogous using an "artificial metal", which is irrefutable as nobody
knows what the characteristics of said "artificial metal" would be. But
I don't think that a planetary explosion based on Deuterium is plausible
enough for a story. I'd put the odds at less than 1 in a google but
greater than 1 in a googleplex per intentional attempt. This, however,
isn't based on calculation, but just because I don't want to say that
it's flatly impossible. (You could possibly increase the odds, but
you'd need to appeal to either extremely large engineering works of a
peculiar nature [a Deuterium mine that encircled the planet?] or some
future discovery in physics. And it would probably still be
unreasonably improbable.)

Try orbital instabilities of the giant planets causing them to collide
in such a way that they dropped into the sun, hitting the Earth on the
way. That's not nearly as fast, but it's more plausible (i.e., not
very), and would be extremely difficult to do anything about. Or
re-read "When Worlds Collide" by Balmer and Wylie. (1930's Science
Fiction, but reasonable enough that a major rewrite could update it.)
