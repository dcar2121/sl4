On Tue, May 27, 2008 at 4:58 PM, Michael Anissimov
<michaelanissimov@gmail.com> wrote:
> Aside from the evaporization of signaling, what about the collapse of
> morality itself? The realization that nothing is inherently worth doing,
> only worth doing according to a certain cognitive configuration? This
> speedbump could be even larger.

Kip Werking has a nice paper on this:
http://transhumanism.org/index.php/WTA/more/Haldane2004/ (section 3.1
specifically)
although I think he overblows it somewhat - hedonic utilitarianism is
not the only ethical theory.

This is hardly specific to transhumanism - God's been dead over a
century. People adapt, sometimes painfully. I don't expect a long-term
problem.
