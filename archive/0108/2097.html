I've been working on this some, so here are some better definitions
I've come up with after putting more of my ideas into words:

At 8:59 PM -0400 8/5/01, Gordon Worley wrote:
>- Sysop
> A mind who's charged with ensuring non violation of volition for
> everyone living in Sysop Space and protect the universe from being
> hosed.

- Sysop
     A mind who's charged with the upkeep of Sysop Space and thus Unix
     Reality.

>- Unix Reality
> An analogy to what reality will be like in Sysop Space. Referes
> to the way that the Sysop will probably act like parts of POSIX.

- Unix Reality
     The system of Sysop Space that ensures the non violation of
     volition with assistance from the Sysop (i.e. the Sysop is the
     source of intelligent decision making when needed). So named
     because of its resemblance to parts of POSIX.

These changes make it clearer that the Sysop is not the system, but
the intelligence behind the system.
