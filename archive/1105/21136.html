> To summarize: *no one is using SL4*. If a ML is not being used, why

I'm not greatly against shutting the list*, but I don't really understand why
you'd ask this question rather than its converse. If a ML is not being used,
and therefore presents no cognitive or server load, why bother inactivating
it? It's a potentially useful thing that would require work to dispose of.

* But, I am greatly against losing the archives. Just to be clear. Maybe
they're mirrored on lesswrong.com or somewhere?
