At 11:27 PM -0400 6/29/01, Eliezer S. Yudkowsky wrote:
>As for the movie itself, I'll give the movie four stars; some judicious
>editing could easily have raised this rating to four and half stars.
>Worth seeing.

Personally, I was bored immensely with the movie and can sum it up in
one word: cute. Even my friend whom I dragged along thought it was
boring, and she's about SL1. Anyway, what follows is my rot13'd mini
analysis of AI. I've submitted this to Slashdot, but they haven't
posted a story about it yet. :-( But, that's Slashdot, and I'll
probably just have to post this as a comment anyway.

Although I must agree with Eliezer, this film is not dangerous at all
the way it seemed like it might be. Although David is done all wrong
as far as how to create AI, Eliezer's correct in that it and the
other meccas act the right way. I give it very few stars. Out of
five, I'd give it maybe two. Certain parts of the film were just
done all wrong, especially the warm, fuzzy ending.

BTW, for those of you reading this and thinking, 'has Gordon
forgotten the folks on this list are SL4, hence the list's name?',
this article was written, in part, to expose the flaws of Classical
AI to the casual Slahsdot reader and encourage the spreading of the
meme of Friendly AI.
