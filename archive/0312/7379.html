Robin Lee Powell <rlpowell@digitalkingdom.org> writes:
> On Sat, Dec 13, 2003 at 01:40:34PM -0500, Eliezer S. Yudkowsky wrote:
>> Watch this space for serious developments in some unknown amount
>> of time, my wild guess being a decade, and quite possibly nothing
>> happening if "faking it well" turns out to be AI-complete.
>
> Could you explain that sentence in more detail? "AI-complete" in
> particular.

"AI-complete", by analogy to "NP complete" etc., means "at least as
complicated to solve as the AI problem/requiring solution of the AI
problem as a subset of the task". It is fairly common shorthand
notation.
