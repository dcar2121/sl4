2009/11/23 Luke <wlgriffiths@gmail.com>:

> Finally, there are over 100 neurotransmitters, any combination of which can
> act at a particular synapse.

For many years it was thought that a single neuron released only one
neurotransmitter. With the discovery of cotransmission that is now
known not to be true, but even so no more than a couple of
neurotransmitters are usually involved, and their release does not
always seem to be differentially variable.
