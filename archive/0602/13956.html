If a 'node' of the AGI brain were a web server in one instance, an SQL
server in another instance, or an embedded API in a cell phone, what
difference would it make to the overall "thinking" process?

If we accept that "real-time" to a human-biological brain, "real-time" to a
human-enhanced brain, and "real-time" to a substrate-independant Jupiter(?)
brain are all very different scales, then it should not matter who fires the
most neurons or processes the most simultaneous threads. What matters (to
me) is that some new idea is contributed to the pool of humanity's
knowledge. Along the way, it will be the developers of AGI that are making
the contribution. 'Success' will be when the AGI makes its first
significant contribution.
